STORY SO FAR...

While young I had a few odd jobs. The one I believe remains the most relevant from that period was working as a dishwasher stooge for a high-class restaurant in downtown Mexico City.

On the bright side, I learned a lot: patience, perseverance, to be actively aware of people and my surroundings, respect for authority, a thing or two about teamwork, to do things that where beyond the actual job, and to believe in myself. On the other hand I had to deal with an aching back, unfair and unequal treatment, unsanitary situation handling, corrosive chemicals and a really low wage.

It was those difficulties that started me thinking about studying a career. I set my mind to search beyond the minimum wage, to become an expert and to maximize my potential.

The EDINBA (INBA's school of design) offers the bachelor of design degree, which spans 4 years. It encompasses a broad range of design categories (graphic, multimedia, industrial, couture, textile and social design) focusing mainly on the design process, aiming to form design professionals able to tackle any kind of design problems.

In 2019 I graduated from this beacon for design in Mexico, and ever since, haven't stoped practicing, fliping, breaking, mending, adding, shaking, bending, stressing, squashing, ing, ing, ing, the design process. It has become part of myself and my whole worldly experience. I also have attended quite a few useful courses.

During and after college my main income source has been as a Freelance Designer. Working directly with clients has shown me to be outspoken, to put into non-specialized language the design process and to help people I work with get the most out of their own ideas.

It's more easily said than done, people in cities live in a rush, everything needs to be done fast and neatly executed. So I've learned to effectively manage project time, do lightning speed research, always try to be one step ahead in case of contingency set backs, taking on the extra hours and to embrace the unknown.

This need for efficiency and sense of urgency made me an avid learner since college. I'm always reading new theory, learning new techniques, practicing known ones and striving to outdo myself.
